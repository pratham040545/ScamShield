=== SECTION 1: UPI PAYMENT SCAMS ===

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) scams are the most common
financial fraud in India. Scammers exploit victims' confusion
about how UPI works — specifically the difference between
sending and receiving money.

THE FUNDAMENTAL RULE VICTIMS FORGET:
You NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money.
PIN is ONLY required when YOU are sending money.
If anyone asks you to enter your PIN to "accept" or "receive"
a payment — it is 100% a scam, no exceptions.

SCAM 1 — COLLECT REQUEST FRAUD:
How it works:
- Scammer sends a UPI collect request to victim's number
- The notification says "Accept Rs 5,000 from [Scammer Name]"
- Victim thinks they are accepting/receiving money
- Victim enters their UPI PIN to "accept"
- They have actually AUTHORIZED SENDING Rs 5,000 to the scammer
Common trigger messages:
- "Maine aapko Rs 10,000 bheje hain. Accept kar lo."
  (I have sent you Rs 10,000. Please accept.)
- "I'm sending you your refund. Please accept the request."
- "Your prize money of Rs 25,000 is being sent. Approve it."
Reality: Receiving money on UPI is automatic. No approval or PIN needed.
         A "collect request" is always a REQUEST FOR YOU TO SEND MONEY.

SCAM 2 — QR CODE FRAUD:
How it works:
- Scammer poses as buyer on OLX/Facebook Marketplace
- "I want to buy your item. Let me send you advance payment."
- Sends a QR code: "Scan this to receive Rs 2,000 advance"
- Victim scans QR code and enters PIN
- Victim has sent money, not received it
Also used for:
- "Scan to receive your EPF refund"
- "Scan to get your electricity bill refund"
- "Scan this QR to verify your bank account"
Reality: QR codes on UPI apps always DEBIT money from your account.
         There is no such thing as a "receive money QR code" in UPI.
         Real buyers pay directly — they never send QR codes to sellers.

SCAM 3 — FAKE CUSTOMER CARE FRAUD:
How it works:
- Victim has a payment issue and searches customer care number online
- Scammers pay for Google ads or use SEO to rank fake numbers
- Victim calls fake number, scammer poses as official support
- Scammer says: "I need to remotely verify your account"
- Asks victim to install AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or QuickSupport
- Once installed, scammer can see victim's entire screen
- Waits for OTP to appear, uses it to transfer money
Fake numbers commonly found for: Paytm, PhonePe, Google Pay, BHIM
Real customer care numbers:
- PhonePe: 080-68727374
- Google Pay: 1-800-419-0157
- Paytm: 0120-4456-456
- BHIM: 18001201740
Rule: Real customer care NEVER asks you to install any app.
      Real customer care NEVER asks for your PIN or OTP.
      Always find customer care numbers from the official app, not Google.

SCAM 4 — OVERPAYMENT SCAM:
How it works:
- Scammer "accidentally" sends more money than agreed
  Example: Item costs Rs 500, scammer sends Rs 5,000
- "Sorry I sent extra by mistake, please refund Rs 4,500"
- Victim sends Rs 4,500 back
- Original Rs 5,000 payment was fraudulent and gets reversed
- Victim loses Rs 4,500
Common on: OLX, Facebook Marketplace, Quikr
Red flag: Anyone who "accidentally" overpays and asks for a refund
          before you've shipped anything is running this scam.

SCAM 5 — SCREEN SHARING FRAUD:
Trigger messages:
- "Your KYC is incomplete. Share your screen for verification."
- "Our bank executive will guide you through the process via screen share."
- "Download this app so our team can help you remotely."
Apps scammers ask to install:
- AnyDesk (most common)
- TeamViewer QuickSupport
- AirDroid
- RemotePC
Once screen is shared:
- Scammer sees your banking app open
- Waits for transaction OTP to appear on screen
- Uses OTP within 30 seconds to transfer money
Reality: No bank, UPI app, or government agency asks for screen sharing.
         Uninstall AnyDesk/TeamViewer immediately if installed this way.
         Call your bank to freeze account if screen was shared.

HOW TO STAY SAFE ON UPI:
1. Never enter PIN for any reason except when YOU are sending money
2. Never scan QR codes sent by strangers
3. Never install screen sharing apps for "customer support"
4. Find customer care numbers only from official apps
5. If you receive unexpected money — do not spend it, it may be reversed
6. Enable transaction limits in your UPI app settings
7. Report fraud immediately on cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930

=== SECTION 2: OTP FRAUD ===

OTP (One Time Password) fraud is the most financially damaging
SMS scam in India. Scammers need just one OTP and 30-60 seconds
to completely drain a bank account or take over any digital account.

THE ABSOLUTE RULE:
Never share OTP with anyone under any circumstance.
No bank, no government agency, no company representative,
no family member calling on someone else's behalf,
no technical support executive will EVER ask for your OTP.
OTP is like the key to your vault. Sharing it = handing over the key.

HOW OTP FRAUD WORKS — STEP BY STEP:
Step 1: Scammer already has victim's phone number and bank details
        (bought from data brokers, obtained via phishing, or data breach)
Step 2: Scammer attempts to log into victim's net banking or initiate transfer
Step 3: Bank sends OTP to victim's registered mobile number
Step 4: Scammer calls victim immediately, uses social engineering to get OTP
Step 5: Scammer enters OTP within 30-60 seconds
Step 6: Transaction complete. Money transferred. Account emptied.
Total time from call to theft: Under 2 minutes.

SOCIAL ENGINEERING SCRIPTS SCAMMERS USE:

SCRIPT 1 — "Wrong OTP" trick:
"Hello, I'm calling from SBI customer care. I accidentally sent
 an OTP to your number instead of another customer. Could you
 please share that OTP so I can redirect it? It won't affect
 your account at all."
Why it works: Triggers helpfulness. Victim thinks they're helping someone.
Reality: The OTP was sent because scammer is logging into YOUR account.

SCRIPT 2 — KYC verification trick:
"This is HDFC Bank. Your KYC is expiring today. To prevent account
 suspension, I need to verify your identity. Please share the OTP
 sent to your registered mobile number."
Why it works: Combines authority + fear of loss + urgency.
Reality: Banks complete KYC through branches or official apps. Never via OTP over phone.

SCRIPT 3 — RBI compliance trick:
"RBI has mandated all bank customers to complete re-verification
 by end of today. Please share the OTP you just received to
 complete your compliance verification and avoid penalties."
Why it works: RBI authority + regulatory fear + deadline.
Reality: RBI never calls individuals. RBI compliance is never done via OTP over phone.

SCRIPT 4 — Reward/cashback trick:
"Congratulations! You have been selected for a Rs 5,000 cashback
 from your bank. To credit the amount, please share the OTP
 sent to your number for verification."
Why it works: Greed + excitement bypasses skepticism.
Reality: Cashback is credited automatically. No OTP needed to receive money.

SCRIPT 5 — Reciprocity trick:
Scammer first sends Rs 1 or Rs 5 via UPI to victim's account.
Then calls: "I just sent you a test payment. Please share the OTP
             you received to confirm receipt on our system."
Why it works: Victim feels obligated after receiving something.
Reality: Receiving UPI payment never generates an OTP. The OTP
         was triggered by scammer attempting a LARGE transaction.

SIM SWAP FRAUD (ADVANCED OTP THEFT):
Scammer doesn't need to call you — they steal your SIM entirely.
How it works:
Step 1: Scammer collects your personal details via phishing or social media
Step 2: Visits mobile operator store with fake ID documents
Step 3: Requests SIM replacement claiming "lost SIM"
Step 4: Your SIM is deactivated. Scammer's SIM gets your number.
Step 5: All OTPs for your bank accounts now go to scammer's phone
Step 6: Scammer logs into all your accounts, transfers money
Warning signs of SIM swap:
- Your phone suddenly shows "No Service" or "SIM not registered"
- You stop receiving calls and SMS unexpectedly
- You receive SMS saying your SIM has been changed
What to do: Immediately call your operator from another phone.
            Call your bank to freeze accounts if SIM swap suspected.

WHAT TO DO WHEN YOU RECEIVE A SUSPICIOUS OTP:
1. Do NOT share it with anyone calling you
2. If you didn't initiate any transaction — someone is trying to access your account
3. Immediately log into your bank app and check for pending transactions
4. Change your net banking password immediately
5. Call official bank helpline to report unauthorized login attempt
6. If OTP was already shared: call bank immediately to freeze account
   Cyber crime helpline: 1930
   Online complaint: cybercrime.gov.in

=== SECTION 3: WHATSAPP ACCOUNT TAKEOVER ===

WhatsApp account takeover is uniquely dangerous because it turns
the victim into an unwitting weapon against their own contacts.
The scammer uses the victim's trusted identity to defraud
family members and close friends who have no reason to be suspicious.

HOW WHATSAPP REGISTRATION WORKS (AND HOW IT'S EXPLOITED):
WhatsApp ties each account to a phone number.
To register WhatsApp on a new device, a 6-digit OTP is sent
to the registered phone number for verification.
Scammers exploit this by:
Step 1: Entering victim's number in WhatsApp on their own device
Step 2: WhatsApp sends 6-digit registration OTP to victim's phone
Step 3: Scammer calls victim and tricks them into sharing the code
Step 4: Scammer enters the code — victim's WhatsApp transfers to scammer's device
Step 5: Victim is logged out. Scammer has full access to all chats and contacts.

TRICK 1 — "YOUR CODE CAME TO MY PHONE BY MISTAKE":
Most common WhatsApp takeover script.
Scammer: "Hi, I'm [friend's name]. I was trying to register and a code
          accidentally went to your number. Can you share the 6 digits?
          It won't affect your account, it's for mine."
Why it works: Sounds completely plausible. Triggers helpfulness.
Reality: WhatsApp codes are sent to YOUR number for YOUR account registration.
         Sharing it = handing over YOUR WhatsApp to the scammer.
         There is no way a registration code for someone else's account
         accidentally lands on your phone.

TRICK 2 — FORWARD THIS MESSAGE TO STAY VERIFIED:
Viral WhatsApp forwards claiming:
"WhatsApp is upgrading to WhatsApp Gold / WhatsApp Plus / WhatsApp Business.
 To keep your account active, forward this message to 10 contacts
 and enter this verification code: [6 digits]"
Reality: There is no WhatsApp Gold. WhatsApp never sends codes via forwards.
         The 6-digit code in the message is an actual OTP for account registration.
         Entering it registers your account on a scammer's device.

TRICK 3 — WHATSAPP WEB QR CODE SCAM:
Scammer sends a message: "WhatsApp is updating security settings.
 Scan this QR code to verify your account and avoid suspension."
Or in person: Scammer shows their phone screen asking you to scan "a payment code"
The QR code is actually a WhatsApp Web linking code.
Scanning it links YOUR WhatsApp to THEIR device silently.
You stay logged in and notice nothing.
Scammer can now read all your messages without your knowledge.
Reality: Never scan WhatsApp QR codes from unknown sources.
         WhatsApp never asks you to scan codes for verification via messages.

WHAT HAPPENS AFTER TAKEOVER:
Once scammer has your WhatsApp:
1. They export your contact list
2. They message your closest contacts pretending to be you
3. Common script: "Yaar bahut emergency hai. Mera wallet kaam nahi kar raha.
   Rs 5,000 bhej de, kal wapas kar dunga." (Friend emergency money request)
4. Contacts send money without suspecting anything — it's your number
5. Scammer targets 10-20 contacts within an hour before you notice
6. Total damage: Rs 50,000-2,00,000 across multiple victims

HOW TO KNOW YOUR WHATSAPP IS HACKED:
- Contacts call asking why you're asking for money
- You get logged out of WhatsApp suddenly
- WhatsApp shows "This account is registered on another device"
- You see messages in chats you didn't send
- WhatsApp Web shows active sessions you don't recognize

IMMEDIATE STEPS IF HACKED:
1. Re-register WhatsApp with your number immediately
   (this logs out the scammer automatically)
2. Enable Two-Step Verification: Settings → Account → Two-Step Verification
3. Broadcast message to all contacts: "My WhatsApp was hacked.
   Ignore any money requests from my number in last [X] hours."
4. Alert close contacts personally — especially those who may have sent money
5. File complaint: cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930

PREVENTION — TWO-STEP VERIFICATION:
Enable this immediately — it adds a 6-digit PIN to WhatsApp registration.
Even if scammer gets your OTP, they can't register without this PIN.
How to enable:
WhatsApp → Settings → Account → Two-Step Verification → Enable
Choose a PIN you will remember. Add recovery email.
This single step prevents 99% of WhatsApp takeover attempts.

LINKED DEVICES AUDIT:
Check if any unknown devices are linked to your WhatsApp:
WhatsApp → Settings → Linked Devices
If you see any device you don't recognize → tap it → Log Out
Do this audit once a month as a security habit.

=== SECTION 5: DIGITAL ARREST SCAMS ===

Digital arrest scams are the most sophisticated and financially
devastating fraud in India. Victims are psychologically trapped
for hours or days by scammers impersonating law enforcement,
resulting in losses ranging from Rs 5 lakh to Rs 2 crore.
Prime Minister Modi specifically warned about this scam
in Mann Ki Baat (October 2024) calling it a national threat.

WHAT IS A "DIGITAL ARREST":
There is NO such thing as a "digital arrest" in Indian law.
It is a completely fabricated concept invented by scammers.
No law enforcement agency in India — CBI, ED, police, TRAI,
Narcotics Control Bureau, Supreme Court — can arrest you
digitally or keep you on a video call as "custody."
If anyone claims you are under "digital arrest" — it is 100% a scam.

HOW THE SCAM WORKS — STEP BY STEP:

STEP 1 — THE INITIAL CALL (creates shock):
Victim receives call from someone claiming to be:
- TRAI officer: "Your mobile number is being used for illegal calls"
- FedEx/courier officer: "A parcel in your name contains drugs/fake passports"
- CBI officer: "Your Aadhaar has been linked to a money laundering case"
- Narcotics officer: "Your bank account received proceeds from drug trafficking"
The claim is always shocking and specific enough to seem credible.

STEP 2 — ESCALATION TO "SENIOR OFFICER" (establishes authority):
"This is a serious matter. I am connecting you to my senior officer."
Call is transferred to another scammer playing a "senior CBI/ED officer."
This person is more aggressive, more official-sounding.
They have your name, address, Aadhaar number (bought from data brokers).
Having personal details makes everything seem terrifyingly real.

STEP 3 — VIDEO CALL IN FAKE "POLICE STATION" (visual deception):
Victim is asked to switch to WhatsApp/Skype video call.
Scammer appears in a police or CBI uniform (bought online for Rs 200).
Background shows a fake "police station" or "court" setup.
Official-looking documents with victim's name are shown on screen.
"Arrest warrant" with victim's photo is displayed.
Visual authenticity breaks down last traces of skepticism.

STEP 4 — ISOLATION (cuts off all help):
"You are under digital surveillance. Do not tell anyone about this call.
 If you contact family, friends, or a lawyer, you will be immediately arrested.
 Stay on this call. Do not leave your room."
Victim is kept isolated for hours or entire days.
Phone is monopolized — victim cannot call for help.
This is the most critical phase — isolation enables everything that follows.

STEP 5 — FAKE LEGAL PROCESS (simulates legitimacy):
Scammers conduct fake "interrogation" asking detailed personal questions.
A fake "Supreme Court judge" joins the video call.
Fake "case number" and "FIR number" are provided.
Victim is told they can be "cleared" by cooperating fully.
Hours of this process make the victim feel deep inside a real legal case.

STEP 6 — THE MONEY DEMAND (the real goal):
"To avoid arrest and clear your name, you must pay a refundable
 security deposit to the Supreme Court account."
"This amount will be returned after investigation is complete."
"Pay Rs [X] lakh immediately to avoid being taken into custody."
Amounts demanded: Rs 50,000 to Rs 2 crore depending on victim's profile.
Payment methods: NEFT to "government account", crypto, gift cards.
Victim pays because they are completely broken down psychologically.

REAL CASES IN INDIA:
- Mumbai retired engineer lost Rs 1.06 crore over 3 days (2024)
- Delhi doctor kept on video call for 40 hours, lost Rs 59 lakh
- Hyderabad IT professional lost Rs 1.7 crore, took personal loan
- Pune teacher lost life savings of Rs 14 lakh in single afternoon
Common pattern: Victims educated, employed, rational people in normal life.
               Fear and isolation bypass education and intelligence entirely.

AGENCIES COMMONLY IMPERSONATED:
- CBI (Central Bureau of Investigation)
- ED (Enforcement Directorate)
- TRAI (Telecom Regulatory Authority of India)
- Narcotics Control Bureau (NCB)
- Cyber Crime Division
- Supreme Court of India
- Income Tax Investigation Wing
- Customs Department
Reality: None of these agencies conduct arrests via phone or video call.
         Real investigations involve physical notices delivered in person.
         Real warrants are served physically by uniformed officers at your door.

HOW TO IMMEDIATELY RECOGNIZE THIS SCAM:
1. Unexpected call about a crime you know nothing about
2. Immediate escalation to "senior officer"
3. Request to switch to video call
4. Person in uniform on video call
5. Told to stay on call and not tell anyone
6. Asked to pay money to avoid arrest
Any ONE of these = hang up immediately.

WHAT TO DO IF YOU RECEIVE THIS CALL:
1. Hang up immediately — do not engage, do not argue
2. Call a trusted family member or friend RIGHT NOW
3. Call the real agency's official number to verify
   (CBI: 011-24368638, Cyber Crime: 1930)
4. Remember: No real officer will stop you from calling a lawyer
5. File complaint: cybercrime.gov.in or nearest police station
6. If you already paid: call 1930 immediately, banks can sometimes reverse

THE THREE THINGS TO REMEMBER ALWAYS:
1. Digital arrest does not exist in Indian law — it is fiction
2. Real officers never demand payment to avoid arrest
3. Real investigations always allow you to contact a lawyer
