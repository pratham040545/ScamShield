================================================================
SCAMSHIELD KNOWLEDGE BASE — FAKE JOB OFFERS
================================================================
This file teaches the AI how fake job offer scams work.
It covers advance fee fraud, fake offer letters, overseas
job scams, and verification methods — with Indian context.
================================================================

=== SECTION 1: HOW FAKE JOB OFFERS WORK ===

Fake job offer scams are uniquely effective because they exploit
hope rather than fear. Victims actively cooperate because they
want the job to be real. This makes fake job scams harder to
detect than other fraud types — the victim's own desire to
believe overrides their skepticism.

THE FIVE STAGES OF A FAKE JOB SCAM:

STAGE 1 — TARGETING AND VICTIM SELECTION:
Scammers systematically identify vulnerable job seekers using:
- Naukri.com: searching "fresher" + "0-1 years experience" profiles
- LinkedIn: filtering recent graduates, people "Open to Work"
- Internshala: targeting students looking for internships
- Shine.com, Monster.com: mass downloading resumes
- College placement groups on WhatsApp and Telegram
- Facebook groups: "Jobs in Mumbai/Delhi/Bangalore"

What scammers look for in victim profiles:
- Fresher or 0-2 years experience (desperate, less experienced)
- Currently unemployed (visible on LinkedIn "Open to Work" badge)
- Skills in high demand (Data Science, IT, Digital Marketing)
- Located in Tier 2/3 cities (fewer local opportunities)
- Recently uploaded resume (actively searching)

Your resume on Naukri or LinkedIn is publicly visible.
Scammers scrape these platforms daily for fresh targets.

STAGE 2 — THE APPROACH:
First contact comes through unexpected channels.
Common approach methods:
- WhatsApp message from unknown number
- LinkedIn InMail from recently created profile
- Email to address listed on resume (often personal Gmail)
- Telegram message
- SMS claiming to be from HR

Sample first message:
"Hi [Your Name], I came across your profile on Naukri.
 We have an exciting opportunity for a Data Analyst role
 at [Fake/Stolen Company Name]. The CTC is Rs 4.5-6 LPA.
 It's a work from home position. Are you interested?
 Please reply to discuss further."

Why it works:
- Uses your real name (scraped from resume)
- Mentions a real-sounding company
- Salary is attractive but believable
- WFH makes it seem modern and flexible
- No pressure — just "are you interested?"

Red flags at this stage:
- You never applied for this job
- Contact from personal WhatsApp/Gmail not official company email
- Company name is vague or recently formed
- No job posting visible on company website or job portals

STAGE 3 — THE FAKE INTERVIEW:
Designed to feel real enough to build confidence.
How fake interviews work:
- Conducted over WhatsApp voice call, Google Meet, or Zoom
- Interviewer uses professional-sounding name and title
- Questions are generic and easy: "Tell me about yourself"
- No technical questions for technical roles
- Interview lasts 10-20 minutes
- Selection is announced within hours or same day

Real interview red flags:
- Selected immediately without any technical assessment
- Interviewer cannot answer basic questions about the company
- Interview conducted only on WhatsApp, not official platform
- Interviewer's LinkedIn doesn't exist or was created recently
- No HR portal, ATS (Applicant Tracking System), or official process

Legitimate hiring process for comparison:
- Application through official portal or job board
- Multiple rounds: screening + technical + HR
- Takes days to weeks, not hours
- Official company email for all communication
- Offer letter comes after reference and background checks

STAGE 4 — DOCUMENT AND DATA COLLECTION:
Even if no money is extracted, this stage alone causes harm.
Documents commonly requested:
- Aadhaar card (front and back)
- PAN card
- 10th and 12th marksheets
- Graduation certificate
- Bank account details (account number + IFSC)
- Cancelled cheque
- Passport size photograph
- Signature on blank paper

What scammers do with these documents:
Identity fraud: Opening bank accounts in your name
Loan fraud: Taking loans using your identity
SIM swap: Getting new SIM on your number
Fake companies: Registering businesses using your details
Dark web: Selling your complete KYC package

Critical rule: Never send identity documents to unverified employers.
              Legitimate companies conduct background checks through
              official agencies AFTER you join, not before offer letter.

STAGE 5 — MONEY EXTRACTION:
The final stage where financial fraud occurs.
Common money extraction methods:

Method A — Direct fee demand:
"To confirm your joining, pay Rs 2,000 registration fee."
"Training kit and laptop will be sent after Rs 5,000 deposit."
"Background verification fee of Rs 1,500 is required."
"Security deposit of Rs 10,000 refundable after 6 months."

Method B — Indirect extraction:
"Buy this course (Rs 3,000) mandatory for the role."
"Purchase our certified software license (Rs 4,500)."
"Join our mandatory pre-joining training (Rs 8,000)."

Method C — Silent identity fraud:
No money asked directly.
Documents used to open accounts, take loans, commit fraud.
Victim discovers months later when bank/loan notices arrive.

THE ABSOLUTE RULE:
No legitimate employer anywhere in the world charges
candidates money to give them a job.
Registration fees, training fees, security deposits,
background check fees, software fees — all are scams.
Legitimate employers bear all hiring costs themselves.

=== SECTION 2: RED FLAGS IN FAKE JOB OFFERS ===

Fake job offers contain red flags at four levels — the initial
contact, the company details, the offer itself, and what the
scammer asks from the victim. Detecting red flags early,
before emotional investment builds, is critical.

LEVEL 1 — RED FLAGS IN THE INITIAL CONTACT:

How they reached you:
- Contacted you without any job application from your side
- Message received on WhatsApp from unknown number
- Telegram DM from unknown account
- Email from Gmail, Yahoo, or Hotmail (not company domain)
- LinkedIn message from profile created recently (check joining date)
- SMS from unknown number claiming to be HR

How they write:
- Generic greeting: "Dear Candidate" instead of your name
- OR uses your name but everything else is generic/copy-pasted
- Poor grammar, spelling mistakes in "professional" communication
- Excessive use of capital letters: "URGENT HIRING", "IMMEDIATE JOINERS"
- Overly enthusiastic tone for a first message
- Copy-paste formatting errors visible in the message

What they say:
- "We found your profile" without specifying where exactly
- Immediate salary mention in first message (real recruiters discuss this later)
- "Work from home" + "No experience needed" + "High salary" together
- "Urgent requirement" or "Immediate joining" pressure
- "Limited seats available" creating artificial scarcity
- Job description is vague: "computer work", "online tasks", "data work"

LEVEL 2 — RED FLAGS ABOUT THE COMPANY:

Company identity:
- Company name is vague: "ABC Solutions", "Global Tech Services"
- Company name sounds like a real company but slightly different
  Example: "Infosys BPO Solutions" vs real "Infosys BPM"
- No verifiable presence: no LinkedIn page, no Google results
- Website was created recently (check with whois.domaintools.com)
- Website has no physical address or shows a residential address
- Website has no employee profiles, About Us is generic

Company verification failures:
- CIN (Company Identification Number) not found on MCA portal
- GST number not verifiable on GST portal
- "We are partnered with Amazon/Google/Microsoft" but no proof
- Claims to be MNC but has no international presence online
- Glassdoor/AmbitionBox shows no reviews or very recent fake reviews

Communication channels:
- All communication on WhatsApp only, no official email
- Official-looking email domain registered recently
  Example: hr@infosys-bpo-solutions.com registered 2 months ago
- Phone number is a personal mobile, not an office landline
- No office address given, or address is a coworking space

LEVEL 3 — RED FLAGS IN THE JOB OFFER ITSELF:

The role:
- Job title is inflated for a fresher: "Senior Manager", "Team Lead"
- Role description is extremely vague: "handle online operations"
- Skills required don't match the role described
- No mention of reporting structure, team size, or projects
- "Any graduate can apply" for a supposedly technical role

The salary:
- Salary is unrealistically high for the role and experience level
  Fresher Data Analyst in India: Rs 2.5-5 LPA realistic
  Fake offers often promise: Rs 6-15 LPA for zero experience
- Salary range is extremely wide: "Rs 3-12 LPA" (real offers are specific)
- "Incentives up to Rs 50,000/month" with no base salary clarity
- Salary mentioned before any interview has happened

The selection process:
- Selected after a 10-minute WhatsApp call
- No technical test for a technical role
- "You are selected" within hours of first contact
- Interview conducted by someone who seems to know nothing about the field
- No background check, reference check, or document verification process
- Joining date is extremely urgent: "Can you join tomorrow?"

The offer letter:
- Arrives within hours of "interview"
- No CIN or company registration number on letterhead
- Designation is "Senior" for a fresher with 0 experience
- Salary breakdown has unusual components
- Signatory name has no LinkedIn presence
- PDF is poorly formatted, logo is pixelated or low quality
- Letter asks you to sign and return within 24 hours

LEVEL 4 — RED FLAGS IN WHAT THEY ASK FROM YOU:

Any money request:
- Registration fee to "confirm your seat"
- Training fee for "mandatory pre-joining course"
- Security deposit "refundable after 6 months"
- Background verification fee
- Laptop or equipment deposit
- Uniform or ID card fee
- Any fee for any reason before or during joining
RULE: Legitimate employers NEVER ask candidates for money. Ever.

Suspicious document requests:
- Aadhaar + PAN + bank details before offer letter
- "Send documents on WhatsApp" instead of official HR portal
- Blank signed paper or signature on blank letterhead
- Cancelled cheque before any employment contract
- Documents requested urgently "within 2 hours"
- More documents than a standard background check requires

Personal information requests:
- Mother's maiden name (security question answer)
- All bank account details including net banking credentials
- UPI ID and registered mobile number together
- Emergency contact details unusually early in process

COMBINED RED FLAG SCORE:
If a job offer has ANY of these combinations — it is almost certainly fake:
- Contacted without applying + salary too high + WhatsApp only = SCAM
- Fast selection + fee required + Gmail communication = SCAM
- Vague role + WFH + no experience needed + high salary = SCAM
- Documents on WhatsApp + no office address + new company = SCAM
- Offer letter within hours + joining tomorrow + fee required = SCAM

=== SECTION 3: INDIAN JOB SCAM PATTERNS ===

Indian job scams exploit specific cultural, economic, and
institutional patterns unique to India. Understanding these
patterns allows detection of scams that generic fraud
detectors completely miss.

PATTERN 1 — IT/BPO COMPANY IMPERSONATION:
India's IT sector employs millions and millions more aspire
to join. Scammers heavily impersonate top IT companies.

Commonly impersonated Indian IT companies:
- TCS (Tata Consultancy Services)
- Infosys
- Wipro
- Cognizant
- HCL Technologies
- Tech Mahindra
- Accenture India
- IBM India
- Capgemini India

How IT job scams work:
Fake "TCS off-campus drive" messages circulate on WhatsApp:
"TCS is conducting off-campus hiring for 2024 batch.
 Apply now: [fake link]. Last date: [urgent deadline]."

Fake appointment letter scam:
Victim receives official-looking TCS/Infosys offer letter.
Letter asks for "joining kit fee" of Rs 2,000-5,000.
Or asks to complete a "mandatory online training" for Rs 3,000.

How to verify IT job offers:
TCS: All hiring through NextStep portal — nextStep.tcs.com
Infosys: All hiring through careers.infosys.com
Wipro: All hiring through careers.wipro.com
Cognizant: All hiring through careers.cognizant.com
Rule: If the job didn't come through the official company
      careers portal — it is not a real offer from that company.
None of these companies charge any fee at any stage.

PATTERN 2 — FAKE CAMPUS PLACEMENT DRIVES:
Scammers target final year students during placement season
(August-December) when anxiety around placement is highest.

Common fake campus drive patterns:
- WhatsApp forwards in college groups:
  "XYZ Company off-campus drive. Register here: [fake link]"
- Fake "placement consultancy" claiming tie-up with your college
- "Mass hiring drive" for 500+ positions — unrealistically large
- Registration link collects personal details and "processing fee"

Fake placement consultancy scam:
"We are an authorized placement partner of TCS/Wipro.
 Pay Rs 1,500 registration fee to appear in our exclusive drive.
 Guaranteed interview call within 7 days."
Reality: No legitimate placement consultancy charges students.
         Companies pay consultancies, not candidates.
         "Guaranteed interview" is always a red flag.

PATTERN 3 — GOVERNMENT JOB SCAMS:
Crores of Indians compete for government jobs — making this
a massive target for scammers.

Types of government job scams:

Fake SSC/UPSC coaching scam:
"Insider notes from toppers available. Rs 2,000 for complete
 study material used by candidates who cleared last year."
Reality: No "insider notes" guarantee success. These are
         generic materials sold at premium prices.

Fake government appointment letter:
Official-looking letter claiming selection for:
- Railways (RRB)
- Banking (IBPS, SBI PO)
- SSC (Staff Selection Commission)
- Defence (Army, Navy, Air Force)
- State government departments
Letter asks for "security deposit" or "uniform fee."
Red flags: Real government appointment letters are free.
           Real letters specify date to report to office.
           Real letters never ask for bank transfers.

Fake "quota" job scam:
"We have contacts in [government department].
 Pay Rs 5-15 lakh to secure a permanent government job."
This is both a scam AND illegal — bribery for government jobs.
Many victims lose lakhs chasing these "connections."

PATTERN 4 — OVERSEAS JOB SCAMS:
Gulf countries (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar), Canada, Australia,
and UK are dream destinations for Indian job seekers.
Scammers exploit these aspirations ruthlessly.

How overseas job scams work:
Step 1: "Direct company visa for Dubai. Salary AED 3,000/month."
Step 2: "Pay agent fee of Rs 50,000 for visa processing."
Step 3: "Additional Rs 20,000 for medical and documentation."
Step 4: Money taken. No visa. Agent disappears.
OR worse: Victim travels to find a completely different job
          than promised — sometimes dangerous conditions.

Fake overseas job offer red flags:
- Job guaranteed without any interview
- Agent asks for money before showing visa
- Communication only on WhatsApp, no registered office
- Company name not verifiable in destination country
- Salary is unrealistically high for the role
- "Only 2 spots left — decide today"

Legitimate overseas recruitment process:
- Recruitment through MOIA-registered agents only
  (Check registration: emigrate.gov.in)
- Company-sponsored visa — company bears visa cost
- Formal interview before any offer
- Offer letter on company letterhead with verifiable details
- You can verify the company exists in destination country

PATTERN 5 — FAKE INTERNSHIP SCAMS:
Targeting students on Internshala, LinkedIn, college groups.

Common fake internship patterns:
"Amazon/Google/Microsoft virtual internship program.
 Certificate + stipend of Rs 5,000/month.
 Pay Rs 1,500 enrollment fee to secure your spot."

Reality: Amazon, Google, Microsoft do NOT offer paid internships
         through WhatsApp with enrollment fees.
         Real internships through Internshala are free to apply.
         Real stipends are paid TO interns, not FROM interns.

Fake certificate internship:
Victim pays Rs 500-2,000 for an "internship."
Receives a certificate from a fake company after submitting work.
Certificate has no value — company doesn't exist in industry.
Used to mislead fresh graduates into fake portfolio building.

PATTERN 6 — DATA ENTRY AND FORM FILLING SCAMS:
Extremely common, targeting homemakers and students.
"Work from home data entry. Rs 15,000-30,000/month.
 No experience needed. Just typing work."

How it works:
Step 1: Victim pays "software license fee" of Rs 2,000-5,000
Step 2: Receives "work" — typing random data into forms
Step 3: Submits work, payment is rejected for "quality issues"
Step 4: No payment ever made despite work submitted
OR:
Step 1: Victim is paid for initial small tasks
Step 2: Asked to "invest" in higher paying task tiers
Step 3: Investment increases, then disappears

Reality: Legitimate data entry work pays Rs 8,000-15,000/month.
         No legitimate data entry job requires upfront payment.
         Legitimate platforms: Amazon Mechanical Turk, Appen, Scale AI.

SALARY BENCHMARKS FOR FRESHERS IN INDIA:
Knowing realistic salaries helps identify inflated fake offers.

Role                          Realistic CTC (Fresher)
Data Analyst                  Rs 2.5 - 5 LPA
Software Developer            Rs 3 - 6 LPA
Digital Marketing             Rs 2 - 4 LPA
Content Writer                Rs 2 - 3.5 LPA
Customer Support              Rs 1.8 - 3 LPA
Data Entry                    Rs 1.2 - 2 LPA
HR Executive                  Rs 2 - 3.5 LPA
Sales Executive               Rs 1.5 - 3 LPA + incentives

Any offer promising significantly more than these ranges
for a fresher with no experience is a red flag.
Example: Rs 8 LPA for a fresher Data Analyst with no experience
         is unrealistic and indicates a fake offer.

=== SECTION 4: HOW TO VERIFY ANY JOB OFFER ===

Job offer verification is a systematic 5-step process that
takes less than 10 minutes and can prevent months of financial
and emotional damage. Always verify BEFORE sharing any
documents or paying any fee — no matter how legitimate
the offer looks.

THE FIVE VERIFICATION STEPS:

STEP 1 — VERIFY THE COMPANY EXISTS:

Method A — MCA21 Portal (Ministry of Corporate Affairs):
Every legitimate Indian company must be registered with MCA.
How to check:
1. Go to mca.gov.in
2. Click "MCA Services" → "View Company/LLP Master Data"
3. Enter the company name exactly as given in offer letter
4. Check: Date of Incorporation, Status (must be "Active")
5. Note the CIN (Company Identification Number)
6. If company not found → company does not exist → SCAM

Method B — GST Verification:
Legitimate companies have valid GST registration.
How to check:
1. Go to gst.gov.in
2. Click "Search Taxpayer"
3. Enter GSTIN from offer letter
4. Verify: Legal Name matches company name, Status is Active
5. No GST number or invalid GSTIN → red flag

Method C — Google Search:
Search: "[Company Name] + reviews + Glassdoor"
Search: "[Company Name] + scam + complaint"
Search: "[Company Name] + headquarters + founded"
Legitimate companies have years of search history.
Scam companies have complaints on consumer forums.
Check: MouthShut.com, ConsumerComplaints.in for fraud reports

STEP 2 — VERIFY THE RECRUITER'S IDENTITY:

LinkedIn verification:
1. Search the recruiter's full name on LinkedIn
2. Check: Profile creation date (recently created = red flag)
3. Check: Number of connections (under 50 = suspicious)
4. Check: Work history matches the company they claim to work for
5. Check: Recommendations and endorsements (fake profiles have none)
6. Check: Profile photo using Google Reverse Image Search
   (drag photo to images.google.com — fake profiles use stock photos)

Email domain verification:
Legitimate recruiter email: hr@tcs.com, careers@infosys.com
Red flag emails: tcs.hr.recruitment@gmail.com
                 infosys.hiring2024@yahoo.com
                 hr.wipro.india@outlook.com
Rule: Any recruiter from a major company using Gmail/Yahoo
      for official communication is not a real recruiter.

Phone number verification:
Search the phone number on Truecaller.
Check if number is registered as "HR" or shows a personal name.
Search number on Google — scam numbers often appear in complaint forums.

STEP 3 — VERIFY THE OFFER LETTER:

CIN verification:
Every legitimate Indian company offer letter has a CIN.
Format: U/L + 5 digits + state code + year + 6 digits
Example: U72200MH2010PTC123456
Verify this CIN on mca.gov.in → must match the company name exactly.

Signatory verification:
Search the signing authority's name on LinkedIn.
They should have a verifiable profile with the company.
Call the company's official number (from official website)
and ask if [name] works there in [designation].

Physical address verification:
Google Maps search the office address from offer letter.
Street View the address — is it an actual office building?
Or is it a residential address, empty plot, or fake address?

Document format red flags:
Pixelated or stretched company logo
Font inconsistencies throughout the letter
Missing letterhead elements (CIN, registered address, website)
Incorrect company registration details
Salary components that don't add up mathematically

STEP 4 — VERIFY THROUGH OFFICIAL CHANNELS:

Call the company's official number:
Find the official number from the company's official website
(NOT from the offer letter or recruiter's message)
Call and ask: "I received an offer letter for [role].
               Can you verify if [your name] has been selected?"
Legitimate companies can verify this.
If they say "we don't have such a drive" → SCAM

Check the official careers portal:
TCS: nextstep.tcs.com
Infosys: careers.infosys.com
Wipro: careers.wipro.com
Cognizant: careers.cognizant.com
HCL: careers.hcltech.com
If your application and selection are not visible
on the official portal → the offer is fake

Check official social media:
Real hiring drives are announced on official LinkedIn pages.
Real companies post about hiring on verified social accounts.
If no announcement exists for the drive they claim → red flag

STEP 5 — TRUST YOUR INSTINCTS AND ASK FOR HELP:

Red flags that need no verification:
- Any fee request = STOP immediately, it's a scam
- "Don't tell anyone about this offer" = manipulation tactic
- Extreme urgency "join by tomorrow" = pressure tactic
- "This offer expires in 2 hours" = artificial scarcity

Ask someone you trust:
Share the offer letter with a college senior, professor,
or working professional in your network.
Fresh eyes often catch red flags you missed due to excitement.
Post in trusted communities: LinkedIn groups, college alumni groups.

Report suspicious job offers:
National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930
Report fake job ads to the platform where you found them:
- Naukri: Report Job button on listing
- LinkedIn: Report this job option
- Internshala: Report internship option
Ministry of Labour complaint portal: labour.gov.in

LEGITIMATE JOB SEARCH CHANNELS IN INDIA:
Always use these official channels — never respond to unsolicited offers.

Job Portals:
- Naukri.com (largest in India)
- LinkedIn.com/jobs
- Indeed.co.in
- Internshala.com (internships and fresher jobs)
- Shine.com
- Foundit.in (formerly Monster India)
- Instahyre.com (tech roles)
- iimjobs.com (MBA/management roles)

Government Jobs:
- ssc.nic.in (Staff Selection Commission)
- upsc.gov.in (UPSC)
- ibps.in (banking jobs)
- rbi.org.in/careers (RBI)
- ncs.gov.in (National Career Service)
- sarkariresult.com (aggregator)

Direct Company Portals:
Always apply through official company career pages.
Search: "[Company Name] careers" on Google.
Look for the official website in results — not third party links.

CHECKLIST BEFORE ACCEPTING ANY JOB OFFER:
□ Company verified on MCA portal?
□ Recruiter has verifiable LinkedIn profile?
□ Communication from official company email domain?
□ No fee requested at any stage?
□ Offer letter has valid CIN?
□ Selection process involved actual technical assessment?
□ Applied through official portal or job board?
□ Offer verified by calling official company number?
If any box is unchecked → verify further before proceeding.
